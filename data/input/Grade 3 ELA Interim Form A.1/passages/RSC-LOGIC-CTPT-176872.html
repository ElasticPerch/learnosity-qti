Government is for Kids

by Mike Weinstein

The government is the group of people that makes laws and enforces them. It also provides services for its citizens. In the United States, some of the people in the government are elected, a few are appointed, and most are hired. The federal government is the group that makes and enforces laws for the whole country. It is based in Washington, D.C., our nation's capital.

1 &nbsp; You know what? You are lucky to be reading this! In the early 1900s, many American kids worked eight or more hours a day, six days a week. They couldn't go to school.

2 &nbsp;But some adults said that education is important. They thought all children should go to school. The federal government in Washington, D.C., agreed. It made full-time work by young children illegal, or against the law.

3 &nbsp; Most people don't spend much time thinking about the federal government. After all, most of you don't often see government workers in your school or in your town. But the federal government is a powerful force in our country. When it makes rules, the whole country must obey them.

4 &nbsp; The government's work extends far beyond Washington, in ways that might surprise you.

5 &nbsp; Ever ride in a car? Of course you do! The government builds and maintains many highways and bridges. It makes laws about the safety of cars.

6 &nbsp; Safety, in fact, is an important part of the government's work. For example, it makes sure that your toys are safe. Recently, some electric cars and trucks that kids can ride were catching on fire. The government found out about the fires and recalled the toys. (A recall means that the toy maker must fix the problem if customers return the toy to be fixed.)&nbsp;

7 &nbsp; Delivering the mail is one of the government's most important jobs. The United States Postal Service brings you postcards from traveling friends and takes your thank-you notes to Grandma. Post offices are found in the biggest cities and the smallest villages. Every time you drop an envelope in that blue mailbox, you are using a government service.

8 &nbsp; Have you ever gone camping in a national park? Thank your federal government for making the campsite available. Did you watch Sesame Street when you were younger? Money from the government helped to pay for that program and the public broadcasting system that showed it.

&nbsp;9 &nbsp;&nbsp;Washington, D.C., may be far away from your home and school. Even so, the federal government touches you almost every day of your life.
